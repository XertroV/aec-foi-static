3. Main Content from the FOI Request Documents Relating to the FOI Request

The core of the released documents is Dr. Michael Turner's "Report of Dr Michael Turner" (the supplemental report), which details the methodology of a voter research survey.

  • Purpose of the Research: To provide robust evidence regarding the impact of including a political party named "The New Liberals" in Australian Federal elections. Specifically, it aimed to answer:
    • If "The New Liberals" makes it harder for voters to correctly identify their intended political party.
    • If "The New Liberals" leads to confusion about its relationship with other registered political parties.
  • Research Design and Methodology:
    • The study employed an online, computer-assisted, self-completion, text-based survey with an experimental design.
    • A significant sample size of n=2,036 Australian voters (aged 18+) eligible for Federal Elections was used.
    • Fieldwork was conducted from March 6-9, 2021.
    • Experimental Ballot Papers: Four different mock ballot paper designs were used to simulate varying prominence of "The New Liberals" relative to the Liberal Party:
      • Ballot A (Control): "The New Liberals" not displayed.
      • Ballot B (Test): "The New Liberals" displayed below the Liberal Party.
      • Ballot C (Test): "The New Liberals" displayed above the Liberal Party.
      • Ballot D (Test): "The New Liberals" displayed, but the Liberal Party not displayed.
    • Questionnaire Design: Carefully crafted with clear, plain language, closed questions. Key prevalence questions (like initial vote intention) were placed early to avoid bias, while attitudinal and clarifying questions were later. Question and response option orders were often randomised.
    • Sample Selection & Weighting: Participants were invited randomly from multi-source online panels, stratified by age-sex interlocking and state/territory based on ABS Census data. Post-fieldwork calibration weights were applied using targets from ABS statistics and 2019 Federal election results to ensure the sample was representative. The effective sample size after weighting was approximately n=1,863.
    • Verification of Intent (Crucial for Confusion Assessment): For respondents who initially selected "The New Liberals," a follow-up question (Q24 in Appendix 2) was posed after providing factual information (e.g., leader, no connection to Liberal Party), asking them to confirm or correct their intended vote. This was a direct mechanism to measure if voters had misidentified the party due to the name.
  • Expert Opinion: Dr. Turner, with extensive experience in social, political, and market research, concluded that the chosen methodology was optimal and appropriate for the project's objectives, aiming to provide robust and generalisable quantitative results regarding voter perception and potential confusion.
  • Redactions: While the detailed methodology and questionnaire are provided, the actual findings and statistical results of the survey (in Appendix 6) are almost entirely redacted.
