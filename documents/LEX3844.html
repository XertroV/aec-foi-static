FOI Request LEX3844

Date: 2023

The FOI request focuses on obtaining information related to the procurement and operational details of a "Senate Scanning Solution" for the Australian Electoral Commission (AEC) in 2018.

Documents from the FOI Request:

The following documents were released in response to the FOI request:

  • RFQ Senate Scanning Solution 2018 - Statement of Requirements: This document outlines the detailed requirements and specifications for the Senate ballot paper scanning solution.
  • RFQ Senate Scanning Solution - Work Order: This document serves as the formal contractual agreement for the provision of Capture and Digital Information Services.
  • Standing Offer - Senate Scanning Solution – Schedule 7: While listed, the content of this document was not provided in the released pages, only its title and associated exemptions (s 47, s 47F).

Main Content from the FOI Request Documents:

The primary content is derived from the "Statement of Requirements" and the "Work Order."

Statement of Requirements (RFQ AEC17/149):

  • Core Purpose: To define the requirements for a solution capable of efficiently and accurately scanning Senate ballot papers for counting purposes, crucial for Australian democratic processes.
  • Testing Services: Mandates comprehensive testing activities, including system testing, system integration testing, User Acceptance Testing (UAT), capacity testing, and security and penetration testing, to ensure solution assurance.
  • Security Requirements:
    • Requires the solution to protect all AEC data (at rest and in transit) in accordance with Australian Government security policies, specifically the Protective Security Policy Framework (PSPF) and the Information Security Manual (ISM).
    • Stresses securing the solution against internet-borne threats.
    • Prohibits sharing of user login credentials.
    • Mandates criminal history checks for all personnel working with the solution and requires them to undergo training on various policies, including Information Security, Acceptable Use, Privacy, and Anti-Fraud.
  • Ballot Paper Handling:
    • Acknowledges that ballot papers can vary (multi-piece, taped, handwritten).
    • Details stringent physical handling procedures:
      • Ballot papers are grouped into "Vote Collection Points" and further "Batched" (approx. 50 papers) with unique coversheets.
      • Transport occurs in "Ballot Paper Transport Containers" (BPTC), each holding around 500 papers.
      • AEC staff perform initial sorting (unfolding, flattening, orientation); the vendor is not expected to undertake specialized sorting.
      • Damaged papers are repaired by AEC staff prior to transport.
      • Strict protocols to minimize movement, ensure only authorised personnel access, and prevent mixing of papers from different collection points.
      • Vendor personnel are explicitly prohibited from marking, modifying, damaging, or destroying ballot papers.
  • Ballot Paper Secure Zones:
    • Outlines rigorous security measures for "Ballot Paper Secure Work Zones" (where scanning occurs) and "Ballot Paper Secure Storage Zones."
    • These zones must be physically segregated, with controls such as lockable rooms/cabinets, security guards, mandated signage, 24-hour CCTV monitoring, physical access controls (smart card/biometric), and back-to-base alarm systems when unattended.
    • AEC staff supervision is required in Work Zones at all times during operation.
  • Data Capture and Workflow Software:
    • Emphasizes the critical importance of accuracy, stating that captured preferences must exactly match the voter's recorded preferences.
    • Requires electronic checking for ballot paper authenticity and formality, with informal or non-standard ballot images visually checked.
    • Stipulates that the solution must comply with all relevant sections of the Electoral Act.
  • Electoral Event Support: The solution must maintain readiness to support electoral events on short notice, as the AEC operates on a continuous election readiness model.

Work Order:

  • This document confirms the formal engagement for "CAPTURE AND DIGITAL INFORMATION SERVICES" under a broader Deed of Standing Offer.
  • It explicitly states that the "Statement of Requirements" (RFQ AEC17/149) is an attached and integral part of the Work Order, highlighting its contractual significance.
  • It also notes the application of "Reserved Materials" and "Reserved and Contract Materials" security classifications to the Work Order, indicating the sensitive nature of the contained information.
