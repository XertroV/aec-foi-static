FOI Request LEX3141

Date: 2022

FOI Request Summary: AEC Graduate Program Orientation Materials

This summary outlines the purpose of FOI Request No. LEX3141, the documents released in response, and their main content.

1. Main Purpose of the FOI Request

The FOI request, identified as LEX3141, sought "PowerPoint slides, notes and any materials relating to the AEC graduate program's orientation sessions given to new staff from the beginning of this year's graduate program to date."

2. Documents from the FOI Request

The following documents were released in response to FOI Request No. LEX3141:

  • 2022 Graduate Induction PowerPoint (Dated 28/01/2022)
  • 2022 Graduate Induction Day Schedule (Dated 28/01/2022)
  • Screenshot of the Induction Program detailing compulsory courses (Dated 2/08/2022)
  • Email to 2022 AEC graduates containing useful links and documents (Dated 31/01/2022)
    • Attachment 1: SDD PowerPoint (Dated 31/01/2022)
    • Attachment 2: New mouse settings and shortcut keys (Dated 31/01/2022)
    • Attachment 3: Workstation set up (Dated 31/01/2022)

3. Main Content from the FOI Request Documents

The released documents provide a comprehensive overview of the Australian Electoral Commission (AEC) 2022 Graduate Program induction and related administrative information:

  • 2022 Graduate Induction PowerPoint: This forms the core of the orientation materials, covering:
    • Introduction to the AEC: Overview of the AEC's role within the Finance Portfolio, key Ministers, the Executive Leadership Team, organizational classifications (APS 1-6, EL1-2, SES Band 1), and national office locations.
    • Graduate Program Overview: Details the structure of the 2022 program, including two six-month workplace rotations, an additional election placement, participation in the APSC Graduate Development Program, and the opportunity for advancement to the APS 5 classification upon successful completion.
    • Program Requirements & Performance: Outlines criteria for successful program completion, including a six-month probation period, achieving "Meets Expectations" or "Exceeds Expectation" performance ratings in Individual Performance Plans (IPPs), and active participation in graduate activities and learning requirements. It details the probation reporting timeline and principles of performance management.
    • Roles and Responsibilities: Defines the responsibilities of graduates, supervisors, and the Graduate Coordinator in supporting the program and individual development.
    • Graduate Work Expectations: Lists typical tasks and duties for graduates, such as administrative tasks, analysis, research, project assistance, and preparing briefing materials.
    • Advancement Criteria: Specifies the conditions for advancing to the APS 5 classification, reiterating the probation, performance, and learning & development requirements, along with submitting an advancement application.
    • Administrative Information: Provides guidance on timesheets, leave, flex time, standard hours of work, and notes that graduates are not eligible for study support or higher duties during the program.
    • Security & Conduct: Emphasizes the importance of maintaining a Baseline security clearance, reporting changes to AGSVA, and adhering to the AEC's Social Media Policy, which stresses political neutrality and avoiding public comments that could compromise impartiality.
    • Support Systems: Highlights various support channels available to graduates, including AEC Learning, rotation supervisors, the AEC Service Centre, Employee Assistance Program (EAP), buddies, colleagues, mentors, and the Graduate Team.
    • Practical Steps: Concludes with immediate actions for new graduates, such as meeting supervisors, setting up workstations, logging onto devices, and setting up timesheets.
  • 2022 Graduate Induction Day Schedule: A detailed timetable for Monday 31 January 2022, listing specific times, activities (e.g., introductions from various divisions, welcome from Commissioners, lunch with buddies), presenters, and locations.
  • Screenshot of the Induction Program detailing compulsory courses: An image showing a list of mandatory modules that graduates are required to complete within the AEC Learning system (e.g., National Induction Program, APS Values & Code of Conduct, Workplace Health & Safety, Cyber Security Essentials, Privacy).
  • Email to 2022 AEC graduates containing useful links and documents: A post-induction email thanking graduates and providing direct links to key intranet pages (e.g., AEC Values and Commitments, IT Guides, HR, Social Media Policy) and attaching supplementary materials for workstation setup and mouse shortcuts.
  • SDD PowerPoint (Attachment 1): The presentation slides for the "Introduction to the Service Delivery Division" by the FAC.
  • New mouse settings and shortcut keys (Attachment 2): A practical guide offering tips for optimizing mouse settings and utilizing keyboard shortcuts for efficiency.
  • Workstation Set Up (Attachment 3): Instructions and advice on ergonomically setting up a workstation to ensure comfort and prevent injury.

SECURITY CLEARANCE

It is a condition of your employment to be able 
to maintain a baseline security clearance.

Baseline clearance is valid for 15 years before 
requiring review.

You are required to update any details and 
changes with AGSVA.

As an Australian Government security clearance holder you have a personal obligation to 
inform AGSVA of any significant changes to your personal circumstances.
You should report only those events that may affect your suitability to hold a security 
clearance. Reportable changes include:
•change of name or identity including gender
•changes of address or share-housing arrangements
•changes in criminal history, police involvement and association with criminal activity
•involvement with any individual that may be a security concern
•security incidents
•external business interests, including business activities with overseas individuals and 
entities

Overseas travel
AGSVA will assess any overseas travel as part of your clearance revalidation. You should 
maintain accurate records of any overseas travel and comply with any pre-departure or 
post-travel obligations required by your employer, such as reporting planned travel or 
participating in security briefings. It is your responsibility to familiarise yourself with any such 
requirements.
Updating contact details
It is important for AGSVA to have your current contact details in the event that we need to 
reach you regarding your security clearance. AGSVA encourages you to use your personal 
contact details rather than your work contact details. This ensures you receive all 
correspondence from AGSVA even after a change to your position or employer.
AGSVA uses your contact details to remind you when your revalidation or annual security 
appraisal (for Positive Vetting security clearances) is due, and if we cannot get in contact 
with you, your security clearance may be cancelled.

SOCIAL MEDIA POLICY

In general, APS employees must not make public comment that may lead a reasonable 
person to conclude that they cannot serve the government of the day impartially and 
professionally.

If an employee uses social media in a personal capacity, it is not appropriate to use 
knowledge of AEC business, or information acquired as a result of the employee’s duties or 
role, to comment on any issue as this could create a real or perceived conflict of interest, 
including questions of political neutrality, impartiality or independence of the AEC. 

it is not appropriate for employees to make comments that are, or could reasonably be 
perceived to be:  made on behalf of the AEC or the government  compromising the 
employee’s capacity to fulfil their duties in an impartial and independent manner  so harsh 
or extreme in its criticism of the government, political parties, or the AEC’s policies that the 
action would provide grounds for a reasonable person to conclude that the employee could 
not serve the government of the day impartially  so strong in its criticism of the AEC that it 
could seriously disrupt the workplace  a gratuitous personal attack that might reasonably 
be perceived to be connected with the employee’s employment; or  able to compromise 
public confidence in the AEC or the APS as a whole.

One of the APS Values set out in section 10 of the PS Act is: Impartial: The APS is apolitical 
and provides the Government with advice that is frank, honest, timely and based on the best 
available evidence.

Basically Remember that political neutrality is a requirement for all AEC employees. 
Employees may not publicly engage, or be active in, political affairs, including on social 
media. Don’t comment on anything, don’t post your job on facebook.
