FOI Request LEX6007

Date: 2024

FOI Request Summary

Main Purpose of the FOI Request

The primary purpose of the FOI request was to obtain access to the Constitution of the Victorian Socialists political party. The provided cover sheet indicates that access to the document was granted in full.

Documents from the FOI Request

The central document released is the "CONSTITUTION of the VICTORIAN SOCIALISTS" (Version 1.1), originally adopted on 22/03/2018 and revised on 14/01/2019.

Main Content from the FOI Request Documents

The Constitution outlines the foundational principles, structure, and operational rules of the Victorian Socialists party:

  • Preamble and Aims (Sections 1, 4): The party is dedicated to democratic socialism, aiming to eliminate poverty, inequality, oppression, exploitation, and environmental destruction. Its objectives include fostering solidarity, endorsing and electing candidates, encouraging working-class political engagement, redistributing power, and advocating for specific policies such as a living minimum wage, affordable housing, improved public transport, public ownership of electricity, and a transition to renewable energy.
  • Party Structure and Governance:
    • Officers (Sections 10-15): Defines five key officer roles: President, Vice-President, Secretary, Assistant Secretary, and Treasurer, detailing their respective duties and responsibilities, including financial management, record-keeping, and general administration.
    • Executive Committee (Section 17): Composed of the Officers and up to four Ordinary Members. It is responsible for the proper management of the party's affairs and is subject to the directives of Party Conferences and the Governing Council.
    • Governing Council (Section 18): Consists of the Officers and 20 elected Ordinary Members. It acts as the governing body, determining party policy and activism, and has powers to interpret the Constitution and direct the Executive Committee.
    • Party Conferences (Sections 19, 20): Designated as the supreme decision-making bodies, held at least every two years (or as Special Party Conferences). They are open to all Financial Members, who have the right to speak, move motions, and vote on matters such as political platform, candidate preselection, and constitutional amendments (requiring a 2/3 majority).
  • Membership (Sections 5-9): Outlines eligibility requirements (agreement with aims), the process for application and termination, and the distinction between "Financial" and "Unfinancial" members, specifying the rights and privileges associated with financial membership.
  • Elections and Vacancies (Sections 21-24): Details procedures for party elections (for Officers, Governing Council, and candidates for public office), including the role of a Returning Officer, nomination requirements, and voting methods (optional preferential system). It also addresses how extraordinary or casual vacancies in leadership positions are filled.
  • Financial Management (Section 16, 30): Stipulates that the Executive Committee controls party funds and property, which must be used solely to further the party's objectives. It also ensures that Governing Council members have access to the party's financial records.
  • Electoral Candidates (Section 29): Imposes specific obligations on elected members, including adhering to party positions in voting (with rare exceptions for "conscience votes"), donating any portion of their salary exceeding the average worker's wage to the party, and seeking Executive Committee consent for staff employment. They are expected to conduct themselves in a manner consistent with party aims.
  • Grievance Procedure (Section 27): Establishes a detailed process for addressing concerns or complaints about member behavior, ensuring natural justice, procedural fairness, and due consideration of evidence.
  • Transitional Rules, Amendments, and Dissolution (Sections 28, 31, 32): Includes temporary rules for the party's initial phase, outlines the requirement for a 2/3 majority vote at a Party Conference to amend the Constitution, and details the procedure for dissolving the party and distributing its assets to aligned organizations.
