FOI Request LEX1697

Date: 2022

FOI Request Summary

Purpose of the FOI Request

The Freedom of Information (FOI) request (LEX1697, an internal review of LEX1147) sought:
* All correspondence between the Australian Electoral Commission (AEC) and major social media platforms (Meta, Twitter, Google, LinkedIn, ByteDance/TikTok) from May 18, 2019, to the date of the request.
* The correspondence specifically relates to compliance with the Commonwealth Electoral Act 1918.
* It also includes discussions regarding the upcoming Federal election to be held in 2022.

Documents Released

The released documents consist primarily of email correspondence and related attachments between the Australian Electoral Commission (AEC) and:
* TikTok (ByteDance): Emails from May to November 2021.
* Google: One email dated June 2020.
* Twitter: Emails from June 2020 and December 2021.
* Facebook (Meta): Emails from June 2020 to December 2021, and April 2021.

Key attachments identified in the schedule and referenced in emails include "Electoral communications escalation scenarios" dated August 2021 and October 2021.

Main Content and Themes

The documents reveal ongoing efforts by the AEC's Electoral Integrity and Communications Branch (including the Electoral Interference Taskforce - EIAT) to collaborate with major social media platforms in preparation for the 2022 Federal Election and to ensure compliance with electoral integrity measures. The primary focus is on combating misinformation and disinformation, establishing clear communication channels, and developing protocols for content moderation and escalation.

General Collaboration & Electoral Integrity

  • The AEC engages with platforms to establish "cohesive working arrangements" for content referrals and to acknowledge efforts in identifying and addressing disinformation.
  • A key theme is the development and discussion of "Online Media – Elections Protocols" and "Electoral communications escalation scenarios" templates with various platforms to streamline responses to electoral interference.

TikTok

  • Escalation Scenarios: Discussions and feedback on "Electoral communications escalation scenarios" templates were ongoing in November 2021.
  • Electoral Integrity Declaration: TikTok proposed working with the AEC on a "declaration of electoral integrity" similar to one established in Canada (August 2021).
  • Public Service Announcements (PSA): TikTok offered an "on-platform Public Service Announcement (PSA)" to appear when users search election-related hashtags, with the AEC providing content and hashtags (July 2021).
  • Verified Account: The AEC considered but decided against creating a verified TikTok account for the 2022 election due to limited capacity for content creation, but remained open to it for future elections (July 2021).
  • Fact-Checking Partnership: TikTok confirmed its ongoing fact-checking partnership with Agence France-Presse (AFP) since 2020, which informs their moderation processes.
  • Transparency & Reporting: The AEC participated in a TikTok Virtual Transparency and Accountability Centre tour (June 2021) and established preferred email reporting pathways for escalating concerning content to TikTok (May 2021).

Google

  • One email is listed regarding the "Eden-Monaro by-election" in June 2020, indicating past engagement on specific electoral events.

Twitter

  • Correspondence from December 2021 references "EIAT and Twitter follow up," suggesting ongoing discussions about electoral integrity matters.
  • An email from June 2020 also relates to the "Eden-Monaro by-election," similar to Google.

Facebook (Meta)

  • Electoral Integrity Preparations: Correspondence in December 2021 focused on "Electoral integrity preparations."
  • Escalation Scenarios & Liaison: Discussions in October 2021 centered on "Electoral communications escalation scenarios" and general "FB/AEC/EIAT Liaison," with the AEC requesting a meeting to discuss scenarios.
  • Briefings: Facebook invited the AEC to selected briefing sessions on "misinformation / disinformation" in June and July 2021.
  • New Reporting Portal: A significant focus in June-July 2021 was on initiating a process for the AEC to gain "trusted access" to a new Facebook reporting portal. The AEC sought more information on the portal's functionality and specific requirements for processing requests to integrate it into their election preparation activities.
  • By-election & General Matters: An email from June 2020 concerned the "Eden-Monaro by-election and other matters," indicating broader engagement.
  • Ongoing Support: An initial call in April 2021 aimed to ensure Facebook was "providing the support the AEC needs" in electoral integrity.
