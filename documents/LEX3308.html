FOI Request LEX3308

Date: 2022

FOI Request Summary

Main Purpose of the FOI Request:
The request sought "all communications between the AEC and the ABS" within 12 months of the Australian Electoral Commission's (AEC) initial contact with the Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS) regarding advice on "party membership testing." The request specifically included communications "epistemically related" to this initial contact, meaning those that explain, causally connect, or directly contribute to understanding the full story.

Documents from the FOI Request:
The request resulted in the release of five documents:
1. Document 1: Detailed letter re methodology from ABS to AEC dated 8 August 1996.
2. Document 2: Letter from AEC to ABS dated 28 August 1996.
3. Document 3: Letter and sample table from ABS to AEC dated 4 September 1996.
4. Document 4: Internal AEC minute approving methodology policy dated 9 September 1996.
5. Document 5: Letter from ABS to AEC dated 29 October 1996.

Main Content Relating to the FOI Request:
The released documents detail the consultative process between the AEC and ABS concerning the methodology for testing political party membership. The core content shows the ABS providing advice and detailed methodologies (e.g., via Document 1 and 3, which includes a sample table) to the AEC. The AEC, in turn, engages with this advice (Document 2). Crucially, Document 4, an internal AEC minute, indicates that the ABS's input directly informed and led to the AEC's approval of a "methodology policy" for membership testing. Document 5 suggests ongoing communication related to this advice. These documents collectively demonstrate the ABS's role in advising the AEC on developing its approach to verifying party membership.
