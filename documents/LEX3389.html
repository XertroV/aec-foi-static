FOI Request LEX3389

Date: 2022

Summary of FOI Request Documents

Main Purpose of the FOI Request

The main purpose of the FOI request was to obtain documents related to the Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS) methodology for sampling and testing political party membership lists. This was specifically in the context of a review of the decision to deregister a political party, VoteFlux.Org | Upgrade Democracy!, which raised questions about the statistical validity and application of the methodology.

Documents from the FOI Request

The FOI request yielded the following documents:
* Document 1: Extract of ABS sample size calculator set to a 6% risk of rejecting a valid list. (Published on Disclosure Log)
* Document 2: Advice from ABS Chief Methodologist on sampling and methodology used, dated 29 July 2022. (Published on Disclosure Log)
* Document 3: Letter to Mr Tom Rogers, Australian Electoral Commissioner from Dr Gruen AO, Australian Statistician, regarding non-parliamentary political party membership testing methodology, dated 2 September 2022. (Published on Disclosure Log)
* Document 4: ABS Party membership testing methodology table. (Published on Disclosure Log)
* Document 5: Advice from ABS Chief Methodologist on sampling and methodology used, dated 19 August 2022. (Access in Part, with exemption 47F applied)

Main Content from FOI Request Documents

The core content, primarily derived from the advice from the ABS Chief Methodologist, addresses key aspects of the sampling methodology:

  • Statistical Validity of Sampling: For a sample to allow statistically meaningful conclusions about an entire list (e.g., 4680 names), the sample must be drawn randomly from the whole list. If only a non-random sub-list (e.g., the top 1,650 names) is tested, conclusions can only be drawn about that sub-list, not the entire original list.
  • Sample Size and Error Rates: To test a larger list to the desired confidence levels (probability of accepting an invalid list less than 2%, probability of falsely rejecting a valid list less than 6%), a larger sample size is required. For a list of 4,680 members, an approximate sample size of 564 would be needed, allowing a maximum of 399 denials (non-members). The ABS provides a calculator to determine appropriate sample sizes.
  • Effect of Filtering Names: Filtering names in accordance with ABS methodology (e.g., removing members unlikely to meet requirements) is beneficial to parties. It improves the 'quality' of the list and reduces the likelihood of finding non-members in the sample, thereby decreasing false rejection risks.
  • Purpose of Methodology: The sampling methodology is designed to be a practical and fair method for testing party lists. It aims to encourage parties to maintain accurate membership records and discourages the provision of large "low quality" lists, which would require extensive sampling to control false rejection risks.
  • Context of Application: The advice was provided in the context of reviewing a decision to deregister a political party, specifically addressing questions raised about the sampling approach used for their membership list. It underscored that if the correct sampling size is not adopted for a larger list, the likelihood of false rejection increases.

This document is an email from the Australian Electoral Commission's Legal Services Section, confirming advice from the Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS) Chief Methodologist regarding the methodology for sampling and testing political party membership lists, specifically in the context of the deregistration review of VoteFlux.Org | Upgrade Democracy!

Key points of the advice include:
* Statistical Validity: Drawing conclusions about an entire list (e.g., 4,680 names) requires random sampling from the whole list. Testing a non-random sub-list (e.g., the top 1,650 names) only provides statistical conclusions about that specific sub-list, not the total list, because records outside the sub-list had no chance of selection.
* Sample Size and Confidence: To maintain desired confidence levels (e.g., false acceptance below 2%, false rejection below 6%) for larger lists, a proportionately larger sample size is required. For a list of 4,680 members, the required sample size is approximately 564, allowing a maximum of 399 denials.
* Filtering Names: Filtering names in accordance with ABS methodology improves the "quality" of the list by removing non-eligible members, thereby decreasing the likelihood of finding denials in the sample and reducing the probability of false rejection.
* Methodology Rationale: The sampling methodology is designed as a practical and fair system. It encourages parties to maintain accurate membership records and provide high-quality lists. Using an incorrect sample size for larger lists significantly increases the probability of falsely rejecting a valid list.
