FOI Request LEX3437

Date: 2022

FOI Request Summary

This summary outlines the information released in response to FOI Request No. LEX3437, focusing on the purpose of the request, the documents provided, and their relevant content.

1. Main Purpose of the FOI Request

FOI Request No. LEX3437 sought:
* Policies, guidelines, directives, rules, and procedures governing:
* The process of selecting measures implemented by the Australian Electoral Commission (AEC) to ensure staff adherence to the APS Values, Employment Principles, and Code of Conduct.
* Any limitation, restriction, or prohibition authorized by the AEC on telephone communication of its staff with members of the public, including call filtering.
* Memoranda of understanding (MOUs) setting out arrangements for liaison between the AEC and the Australian Federal Police (AFP) concerning information sharing, complaint handling, and other operational matters.

2. Documents from the FOI Request

The following documents were released in response to the request:
* AEC Procedures for determining breaches of the APS Code of Conduct and Sanctions (December 2018)
* Acknowledgement and declaration of key obligations (Undated)
* Letter of engagement as an ongoing APS employee (Undated)
* APS1 Induction and training booklet (February 2022)
* National Induction Program: Outline (Undated)
* National Induction Program: Curriculum Overview (Undated)
* National Induction Program: Roles and Responsibilities (Undated)
* National Induction Program: Detailed curriculum and associated learning outcomes (Undated)
* Public Engagement Team Handbook (November 2022)

(Note: The schedule of released documents also listed "AEC Intranet page: Dealing with difficult behavior", "AEC Intranet page: AEC values and commitments", "AEC Intranet page: Conditions of employment", and "AEC Intranet page: Key obligations as an APS employee" but these documents were not provided in the given text.)

3. Main Content from the FOI Request Documents

The released documents provide content primarily related to the first part of the FOI request concerning staff adherence to professional standards and limitations on public communication. No documents were provided regarding MOUs with the Australian Federal Police.

a. Measures for AEC Staff Adherence to APS Values, Employment Principles, and Code of Conduct:
  • Formal Breach Procedures:
    • AEC Procedures for Determining Breaches of the APS Code of Conduct and Sanctions outlines the process for investigating suspected breaches of the APS Code of Conduct, including who can determine a breach, the requirement for procedural fairness (informing the employee, opportunity to respond, independent decision-makers), and the selection of proportionate sanctions (ranging from reprimand to termination). Records of determinations and sanctions are required.
  • Obligation Acknowledgement at Engagement:
    • The Acknowledgement and declaration of key obligations form requires staff to acknowledge their commitment to political neutrality, avoiding conflicts of interest, accuracy of provided information, Work Health and Safety (WHS) compliance, and familiarity with relevant legislation (including the Public Service Act 1999) and AEC policies. It specifically references the APS Values, APS Employment Principles, and Code of Conduct as key obligations.
    • The Letter of engagement as an ongoing APS employee explicitly states that engagement is conditional on "Complying with the Australian Public Service (APS) Values and Code of Conduct." It reiterates the requirement for political neutrality and details several points of the APS Code of Conduct (e.g., honesty, diligence, respect, compliance with laws/directions, confidentiality, conflict of interest avoidance, proper resource use, not providing false information, upholding integrity). Failure to meet these conditions can lead to offer withdrawal or employment termination.
  • Induction and Training Programs:
    • The APS1 Induction and training booklet introduces new staff to the AEC's values (Electoral Integrity, Quality, Agility, Professionalism, "Every task matters," and "Ballot paper principles"), the APS Values (ICARE: Impartial, Committed to service, Accountable, Respectful, Ethical), and the full text of the APS Code of Conduct. It emphasizes that Code breaches can lead to sanctions up to termination. It lists mandatory eLearning courses covering WHS, Protective Security, Fraud, Privacy, IT Security, and Ballot Paper Handling, among others.
    • The National Induction Program documents (Outline, Curriculum Overview, Roles and Responsibilities, Detailed curriculum and associated learning outcomes) describe a structured, nationally consistent induction program for APS2-SES staff (APS1 staff are noted as outside its direct scope, but are covered by the APS1 booklet). This program aims to ensure staff behave in accordance with AEC/APS values and the Code of Conduct, promote a positive organizational culture, and comply with policies and legislation. The curriculum explicitly includes modules on APS Values and Code of Conduct, Privacy, Protective Security, Fraud, Political Neutrality, Conflict of Interest, and Public Governance. It defines the roles and responsibilities of Line Managers, Local Induction Advisers, Executive Level Officers, and Trainers in guiding new staff to meet these standards, emphasizing professional conduct and adherence to established procedures.
b. Limitations, Restrictions, or Prohibitions on Telephone Communication with Members of the Public:
  • Public Engagement Team Handbook:
    • Outlines the responsibilities of Public Enquiries Officers, including answering calls to the National Office switchboard and managing public email inquiries.
    • Details contact telephone numbers, hours of operation (9am-5pm, Mon-Fri, diverted to call centre during federal elections).
    • Provides procedures for Interpreter Calls and the National Relay Service.
    • Specifies instructions for Transferring switchboard to an internal number and back.
    • Under "Incoming calls" (Appendix A), it explicitly states:
      • Marketing and sales calls are not to be transferred to staff.
      • Officers may not confirm whether a person works for the AEC, a person’s work number, a person’s email address, or the format of AEC email addresses.
      • For Senior Executive Staff (Assistant Commissioners and above), Public Enquiries Officers do not transfer calls from members of the public; instead, they take a message. For MPs, Senators, major political party executives, or other Government Agency callers, contact relevant Executive Assistants or Branch Project Officers for discussion.
    • Details the AEC's responsibility for recording Recorded messages for switchboard for outside business hours, public holidays, and emergencies, including specific scripts for these messages.
  • APS1 Induction and Training Booklet:
    • Includes a "Public Enquiries" section with guidelines for quality client service, emphasizing a positive and helpful approach, accurate information, and timely resolution.
    • Sets "Service level standards" for public contact channels: Phone (answered within 90 seconds), Walk-in (acknowledged within 5 minutes), Email/Post (responded within 5 working days).
    • Instructs staff to resolve straightforward inquiries at the first point of contact and record them in the Public Engagement Contact Tool (PECT).
    • For more complex inquiries, or those from political parties/candidates, staff are instructed to refer them to their supervisor or the Divisional Returning Officer (DRO).
    • All complaints are to be logged in PECT, acknowledged promptly, and resolved within 10 business days.
c. Memoranda of Understanding with Australian Federal Police:
  • No documents were provided in response to this part of the FOI request.

2       Who may determine a breach of the Code and impose a sanction 
Note:

breaches of the Code, the Agency Head must, before making a decision to deal with a suspected

standards and guidance issued by the Australian Public Service Commissioner.  

Investigations of suspected misconduct by former employees can be undertaken, but sanctions cannot 
be imposed on former employees. 

2.1 

As soon as practicable after a suspected breach of the Code has been identified, the 
Electoral Commissioner or the Deputy Electoral Commissioner may deal with a suspected 
breach, in accordance with the Procedures, by: 

a)  making a written determination whether the APS employee has breached the Code; or 

b)  selecting the person  to make a determination whether the APS employee has breached

Note: 

The Australian Public Service Commissioner’s prior written consent is required if a non APS 
employee will be selected as the breach decision maker / sanction delegate (subsection 78(8)

An internal or external investigator may be appointed by the breach decision maker to 
investigate the alleged breach, gather evidence and present a report of factual findings, 
including recommendations.  

The person making the decision on the sanction must hold a delegation under the Act to do 
so (the sanction delegate). 

Note: These Procedures do not prevent the breach decision maker from being the sanction decision 
maker in the same matter. 

Subject to clause 2.3 above, these procedures do not prevent the breach decision maker 
from being the sanction delegate in the same matter. 

The Australian Public Service Commission publication ‘Handling Misconduct Managers Guide 
’, is a recommended resource available to the Breach Decision Maker and Sanction Delegate, 
to support them in making the determination and sanction decision.

About the AEC 

The AEC’s purpose 

The Australian Electoral Commission (AEC) is an independent statutory authority established by the 
Australian Government and our purpose is to: 

Maintain an impartial and independent electoral system for eligible voters through active 
electoral roll management, efficient delivery of polling services and targeted education 
and public awareness programs 

We do this by: 

■  ensuring confidence in the Commonwealth electoral roll 

■  conducting successful electoral events, including federal elections, by-elections, 

referendums and industrial elections and ballots 

■  undertaking public awareness activities 

■ 

regulating political party registrations and financial disclosure 

■  supporting electoral redistributions1. 

Structure of the AEC 

Three tier structure 

The AEC has a three-tier structure: 

■  a national office in Canberra 

■  state and territory offices, and 

■  divisional offices. 

National office 

There are three divisions in the national office – the Service Delivery Division, the Enabling and 
Regulation Division and the Organisational Transformation Division – headed by the agency's three 
first assistant commissioners. The First Assistant Commissioner, Service Delivery Division is the 
National Election Manager.

State offices 

State offices are located in each state capital. The Northern Territory office is in Darwin. The

Divisional offices

each electoral division is represented by a member of the House of Representatives.

Ballot paper principles 

The AEC upholds the sanctity of the ballot paper in all its forms and at all times. This is given effect 
through two principles: 

■  All ballot papers remain ‘live’ from printing through to statutorily authorised destruction. 

■  The security, integrity and accountability of ballot papers must be preserved at all times – 

including transit and storage by the AEC, contractors, or other third parties. 

These principles underpin all AEC planning, training, systems, handling and practices associated 
with ballot papers. They show how important ballot paper security is and provides a basis for the 
treatment of every ballot paper from printing to destruction by anyone who handles ballot papers, 
including but not limited to staff, polling officials and contractors.

National Induction Program  
Roles and Responsibilities 

Participant 

Completion of the National Induction Program is a requirement as part of the Participant’s 
introduction to the Australian Electoral Commission (AEC) and Australian Public Service (APS) and 
must be prioritised. 

Participants are ongoing or non-ongoing staff that are new to the agency, or who gain promotion at 
an identified level. 

The National Induction Program is designed for APS2 – SES level staff only, APS1 and staff 
identified to fill key election specific roles are outside scope for this program. 

Line Manager 

The Line Manager will be the responsible officer that conducts the National Induction Program 
locally. The Line Manager will either action or delegate induction activities, and provide feedback on 
performance that is in line with the Australian Electoral Commission’s (AEC’s) Performance 
Management Program and Probation Policy.  

In some instances the Line Manager may not be located in the same office as the Participant  
e.g. Participant is an APS6 in a stand-alone office and their Line Manager is an Operations Manager 
in the State Office. The Line Manager will: 

■  provide one on one training and support for the Participant on AEC specific information 

■  use and refer the Participant to approved AEC policies and procedures only 

■  ensure they are familiar with all training content prior to actioning each module 

■  be available to the Participant, responding to questions as needed, ensuring the Participant 

feels comfortable to seek support

follow all guidance in relation to providing the Participant with feedback on their progress and

Local Induction Adviser 

The Local Induction Adviser will be a locally based officer identified by the Line Manager and will be 
responsible for supporting the Participant in their first five months with the Australian Electoral 
Commission (AEC). 

The Local Induction Adviser must have the skills and knowledge to support the Participant throughout 
the National Induction Program, and is a voluntary role. Where the Local Induction Adviser believes 
that they do not have the appropriate skills and knowledge to undertake this task, they must discuss 
this with their Line Manager immediately.

Executive Level Officer 

The Line Manager’s assigned Executive Level Officer must be engaged to ensure that expectations 
of the National Induction Program and the Participant are being met, that mandatory training is being 
completed and that the Participant is fit for the AEC prior to the probation period end date. 

The Executive Level Officer will not participate in the learning and development of the Participant 
(unless the Participant is their direct report) but will be available to the Line Manager as needed. 

The Executive Level Officer may meet with the Participant on their first day, either face to face or via 
skype, to discuss values and expectations of the agency and their state/branch. Meetings will be 
dependent on the Executive Level Officer’s time, other scheduled meetings and Participant role.
