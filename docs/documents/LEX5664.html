FOI Request LEX5664

Date: 2024

FOI Request Summary

This FOI request sought documents related to a 2023 Town Hall meeting, focusing on internal discussions and questions raised during the event, particularly in the context of public service ethics and accountability.

Documents Released:

  • Transcript of 2023 Town Hall - produced by voice to text AI function: Released in part due to s 22(1)(b)(ii) exemption (containing significant redactions).
  • Transcript of Questions at 2023 Town Hall: Released in full.

Main Content from Documents:

The released documents reveal a key discussion point during the 2023 Town Hall was the "Robodebt" scandal and its implications for ethical conduct within the public service.

  • Concerns about Robodebt: An anonymous question explicitly stated, "Some of us have been super concerned watching Robodebt. Could we have an independent ethics adviser, not an AEC SES, so if we need to consult someone we can."
  • AEC's Response to Ethics Concerns:
    • The Moderator (Jeff) responded by acknowledging Robodebt as "terrible" and affirming that no similar behaviour or anything close to it had been observed within the AEC during his 10 years there.
    • For independent advice, Jeff suggested the Australian Public Service Commission (APSC) and highlighted various other avenues for seeking advice or escalating concerns, including anonymously.
    • The Electoral Commissioner, Tom Rogers, also addressed the "Robo debt question," echoing Jeff's sentiment and stating he had "certainly never seen anything like that, any systemic large scale events such as that internally in the AC," even going back 30-40 years in the AEC's history.
    • A follow-up comment by another anonymous user and a moderator's reply further reinforced the APSC's Ethics Advisory Service as a resource, noting that the APS would be doing more work around integrity following the findings of Robodebt.
