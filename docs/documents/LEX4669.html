FOI Request LEX4669

Date: 2023

Summary of FOI Request Documents: Authorisation Concerns for Referendum Communications

Main Purpose of the FOI Request

The FOI request sought access to documents related to communications from the Australian Electoral Commission (AEC) concerning the failure to authorise referendum matter, specifically regarding the "Referendum News" Facebook page. The request aimed to understand the nature of the "breach notice" issued and the subsequent exchanges between the AEC and the involved party concerning authorisation compliance.

Documents from the FOI Request

The released documents primarily consist of eleven entries:
* One email titled "Failure to authorise referendum matter" (Document 1).
* One letter, LEX4124, dated 26 July 2023 (Document 2), which appears to be the "breach notice" mentioned in the emails, though its content is heavily redacted.
* Nine emails (Documents 3-11) titled "Authorisation concerns," which form an email chain between the AEC's Authorisation Section (and Legal Services Branch) and a representative of the entity involved, concerning the breach notice and compliance.

All documents were released in part, with exemptions applied under s 47F (personal privacy).

Main Content from the FOI Request Documents

The documents reveal the following key information related to the FOI request:

  • Initial Notification of Breach: On 26 July 2023, the AEC's Legal Services Mailbox sent an email to the Authorisation Section, enclosing a letter (LEX4124) regarding a "Failure to authorise referendum matter." This letter served as a "breach notice" to an external entity.
  • Engagement from the Breaching Party: On 31 July 2023, a representative of the entity (later identified as "Advance Australia," operating via the "Referendum News" Facebook page) contacted the AEC Authorisation Section, acknowledging receipt of the "breach notice" and seeking to discuss the matter.
  • AEC Guidance on Authorisation:
    • The AEC confirmed that paid communications (ads) on the "Referendum News Facebook page" containing "referendum matter" (expressly promoting or opposing the law) require authorisation under section 110C(1)(a), (c) and (5) of the Referendum (Machinery Provisions) Act 1984.
    • The AEC extended the compliance deadline to 5pm, Monday 7 August 2023.
    • Specific guidance was provided on the notifying particulars for non-printed communications (such as Facebook posts), as per Section 12(2)(d) of the Commonwealth Electoral (Authorisation of Voter Communication) Determination 2021. For such communications, if a URL/hyperlink is not feasible in an image, the authorisation can be placed at the end of the communication.
    • For "other communications" by a "disclosure entity" (not a natural person), the authorisation message must include: (a) the name of the entity, (b) the relevant town or city of the entity, and (c) the name of the natural person responsible for giving effect to the authorisation.
  • Compliance Action Taken: On 5 August 2023, the representative from "Advance Australia" confirmed they had "amended Referendum News and all other assets to reflect our previous discussion and this advice."
  • Further Clarification from AEC: On 7 August 2023, the AEC acknowledged the timely action but noted that the authorisation particulars initially provided by "Advance Australia" for their Facebook posts were suited for printed communications. The AEC reiterated the correct particulars for non-print materials and attached a "Referendum flowchart" for further assistance, indicating it was up to "Advance Australia" to amend their particulars if they wished. The AEC's general guidance emphasized: "when in doubt, authorise it."
