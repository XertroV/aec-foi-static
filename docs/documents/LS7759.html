FOI Request LS7759

Date: 2019

Summary of FOI Request LS7759 Documents: 'Stop Selling Australia' Party Registration

Main Purpose of the FOI Request

The FOI request (LS7759) sought documents related to the attempted registration of the 'Stop Selling Australia' party, specifically focusing on the "member checking phase." The requester aimed to obtain:
* Email responses from each person contacted during membership testing.
* Any notes or recordings from responses received via phone calls.

Documents from the FOI Request

The released documents include:
* Document 1: Extract of AEC website information on ABS's method for testing political party membership.
* Document 2: The completed application form for 'Stop Selling Australia' party registration (5/09/2018).
* Document 5: Email chain regarding membership testing results and updated membership lists (23/01/2019).
* Document 9 & 14: Emails from Roll Management Section (RMANS) to Parliamentary and Party Registration Section (PPRS) detailing results of matching the membership list against the electoral roll (24/01/2019 & 28/02/2019).
* Document 10: The membership list of the 'Stop Selling Australia' party after electoral roll checking (24/01/2019).
* Document 20: Mail merge document with emails sent to a random sample of party members for testing (04/03/2019).
* Document 21: Email chain showing one person's response to membership testing, including a subsequent phone call confirming denial (20/03/2019).
* Document 22: Compiled email responses from membership testing (04/03/2019-16/03/2019).
* Document 23 & 24: Tables summarizing and breaking down the results of membership testing (04/03/2019-20/03/2019 & 20/03/2019).
* Document 25: Internal Minute to the delegate recommending refusal of the application in its current form under s 131 of the Commonwealth Electoral Act 1918 (21/03/2019).
* Document 26: Notice sent to the registered officer of the 'Stop Selling Australia' party under s 131 of the Electoral Act (29/03/2019).
* Document 27 & 28: Notices regarding suspension and recommencement of application processing due to the 2019 federal election (12/04/2019 & 24/06/2019).

Main Content Related to the FOI Request

The documents detail the Australian Electoral Commission's (AEC) process of assessing the 'Stop Selling Australia' party's application for registration, specifically focusing on its compliance with the requirement of having at least 500 members who are on the Commonwealth Electoral Roll.

  1. Application and Membership List: The 'Stop Selling Australia' party submitted an application for registration, including a membership list. After initial checks and subsequent updates provided by the party, a list of 550 members was processed.
  2. Electoral Roll Matching: The AEC's Roll Management Section (RMANS) conducted checks against the electoral roll.
    • An email dated 24 January 2019 showed 406 members matched, 133 had no match, and 11 had multiple matches out of 550.
    • A later email dated 28 February 2019 indicated 404 matched, 135 no match, and 11 multiple matches.
    • Ultimately, after manual identification efforts, 503 members were identified as being on the electoral roll.
  3. Membership Testing (Direct Contact): To verify the membership, the AEC conducted a random sample test.
    • Based on ABS methodology, a sample size of 26 members was required for direct contact, with a maximum of 1 denial of membership permitted for the party to pass.
    • The AEC attempted to contact 52 members in total, as some were uncontactable. Emails (Document 22) were sent to individuals requesting confirmation of membership.
    • Document 21 highlights a critical case: one individual initially provided a seemingly neutral email response, but a subsequent phone call (as per the FOI request's interest in phone responses) revealed they denied being a member of 'Stop Selling Australia'.
  4. Results and Outcome:
    • Out of the contacted sample, 24 members confirmed their membership.
    • However, 2 members denied their membership, exceeding the permitted single denial.
    • Therefore, the 'Stop Selling Australia' party's application "FAILED" the membership testing assessment.
  5. AEC Decision: As detailed in Document 25 (Minute to delegate) and Document 26 (s 131 Notice), the AEC determined that the application, in its current form, did not satisfy the requirements of the Commonwealth Electoral Act 1918, primarily due to failing the membership test. The AEC issued a notice under subsection 131(1) of the Act, advising the party that the application would be refused in its current form but offering the opportunity to vary the application (e.g., provide a new, compliant membership list) to avoid refusal.

Purpose 
This minute asks you, as a delegate of the Electoral Commission, to give notice to the applicant 
of the Stop Selling Australia Party (the Party) under subsection 131(1) of the Electoral Act. This 
notice will give the Party the opportunity to vary the application so refusal of the application may 
not be required. 

Authority 
Sections 4, 123, 126, 129 and 131 and subsections 4(1) and 123(1) of the Electoral Act. 

Background 
On 24 November 2018, the Australian Electoral Commission (AEC) received all of the 
requirements from the Party for an application for registration as a non-Parliamentary party 
under paragraph 126(1)(b) of the Electoral Act (folio 52). The application included a copy of the 
Party’s constitution (folio 16), an electronic list of between 500-550 members and a statutory 
declaration from the proposed Party secr tary regarding the authenticity of these documents 
(folio 21). The Party indicated that it did not wish to register an abbreviation or party logo  
(folio 32).  On 23 January 2019 and 22 February 2019 the Party provided updates to its 
membership list which impacted on  he  ime taken to assess the application.  

Initial assessment of application 
In the case of a non-Parliamen ary party paragraph 126(1)(b) of the Electoral Act provides that 
the application may be made by 10 members of the party, of whom one is the secretary of the 
party. The application is made by 10 members of the Party, one of which is the Party secretary, 
and sets out the names and addresses of the applicants, and particulars of the capacity in which 
each applicant makes the application (folio 30).  

Assessment of constitution 
Sections 4 and 123 of the Electoral Act define an eligible political party as an organisation the 
object or activity, or one of the objects or activities, of which is the promotion of the election to 
the Senate or to the House of Representatives of a candidate or candidates endorsed by it.  

Subsection 123(1) of the Electoral Act defines an eligible political party as a political party that is 
“established on the basis of a written constitution (however described) that sets out the aims of 
the party”.
